ABCDEmail Search Advertising Analytics Project Project

Feb – Mar 2026 · Los Angeles, CA
Excel Regression Analysis PPC / Search Ads Keyword Analysis Budget Optimization

Business Problem

The paid search strategy was generating conversions at a cost higher than their economic value, resulting in sustained campaign losses. Performance varied significantly across keywords, match types, and time windows, indicating structural inefficiencies in bidding and keyword selection. A data-driven optimization strategy was needed to eliminate unprofitable segments and improve overall return on advertising spend.

Outcome

Identified a $282,953 net loss driven by a CPA of $172 against a $100 break-even threshold. Revealed that unbranded keywords operated at 4× the CPA of branded terms, and built regression models to surface statistically significant high-intent keywords for targeted bid increases.

What I Did

  • Quantified overall campaign profitability by aggregating revenue, cost, and cost-per-conversion, identifying a $282,953 net loss and CPA ($172) above the $100 break-even threshold
  • Conducted keyword-level profitability analysis to distinguish revenue-generating keywords from loss-driving variants
  • Segmented performance by branded vs. unbranded keywords, revealing branded terms as profitable ($53.96 CPA) and unbranded terms as structurally unprofitable ($229.86 CPA)
  • Evaluated match-type efficiency (Exact, Broad, Phrase) and ranked performance based on cost-per-conversion
  • Analyzed hour-of-day performance to assess temporal bidding efficiency, identifying mid-day as relatively strongest but still unprofitable
  • Built fixed-effects regression models for conversions and clicks to control for keyword-specific unobserved heterogeneity
  • Identified statistically significant baseline performers and recommended bid increases for high-intent keywords
